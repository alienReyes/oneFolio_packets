Challenge Problem 2.1: Predicting Volcanic Eruptions

Volcanic eruptions are powerful, seismic events that have devastating effects both locally and globally. Lava flows and the massive terrain deformation are immediate and significant dangers to those in the vicinity of an eruption, but other dangers extend globally. Massive eruptions can trigger “volcanic winters,” as ash clouds spewed into the atmosphere darken the sky and lower temperatures around the world.

The Challenge:

The non-profit company Agri(Culture)2 for which you are consulting, has received a grant to purchase land for a collective, community-based agribusiness. Luckily, a perfectly-sized coffee estate near Santa María has become available. The volcanic soil is perfect for coffee cultivation, and the trees on the estate are approximately 5 years old, meaning that they will begin to mature at about the same time you could complete the purchase and establish the agribusiness.

The local community fully supports this project, but the organization is hesitant to devote a substantial investment of time and money to a business located on an active volcano. Locals still speak of the 1902 eruption that devastated the area and killed more than 5,000 people. You have been asked to determine if the risks outweigh the rewards. According to their projections, Agri(Culture)2 is convinced that the venture will succeed if at least the first five years of operations are free from volcanic eruptions. Follow the steps below to determine the probability of Santa María erupting during this time.

  1. Determine the correct value of λ for the volcano Santa María by identifying the number of significant volcanic eruptions that have occurred since its first eruption in 1902.
  2. Calculate v(0) and explain what the value represents. Hint: You may need to research the value of 0!, which is read “zero factorial.”
  3. Explain the significance of the value 1 – v(0). Hint: Remember that v(k) returns a probability value that is between 0 and 1.
  4. Explain why 1 – e–λ calculates the probability of an eruption during one year.
  5. The expression 1 – e–tλ, which is slightly modified from the expression in part (d), calculates the probability of a volcano erupting during a t-year period of time. Apply this expression to calculate the probability of Santa María erupting during the next five years.
